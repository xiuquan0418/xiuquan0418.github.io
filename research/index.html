Domain adaptation

The incredible success of deep learning shows the potential of artificial intelligence to learn from complex large-scale whole-genome data in computational biology. However, it can be extremely expensive or time-consuming to collect and annotate data in biology. In the lack of sufficient data, biologists always leverage probabilistic modeling to generate datasets. However, deep learning-based attempts using simulated datasets always struggle with model misspecification, as real data often has additional noise which is not well-characterized in probabilistic modeling. Our ongoing work is focusing on proposing new domain adaptation methods to fill the gap between probabilistic modeling and real data.
